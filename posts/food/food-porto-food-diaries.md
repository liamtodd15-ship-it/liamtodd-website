Bifana. Picanha. Rojão.

The holy trinity of Portuguese x Brasilian food. 

Every time I go to Porto to see Vic, we eat like dinosaurs. It often revolves around simple meats, salads, rice and beans. It's basic. You think you can make it at home, but you won't ever come close.

Bifana. A traditional Portuguese sandwich, filled with marinated, juicy, slightly spicy, thinly sliced pieces of pork. They're soft and tender, and very easy to eat two of them as a snack inbetween lunch and dinner. I have almost no idea what the marinade is, but I do know that it's cooked for so long is basically falls apart in your mouth. If you're paying over €3, you're getting ripped off.

Picanha. This might be controversial, but I've only had picanha at the infamous shopping mall restaurant, Fogo de Chão. And I'm not mad about it. The amount of delicious food you get at this place is hard to beat. I'd love to go to a proper steak restaurant and have this sliced tableside, a boy can dream.

Rojão. Saving the best to last. It's similar to the meat in a bifana, but it's still a whole piece. Casa Expresso is truly the only place in Porto to get Rojão that you'll die for, it is the dictionary definition of a hidden gem. It is filled to the brim with locals every night of the week and the interior doesn't feel like it's been updated since the refurb in '84. Served up with their  delicious house salad, rice and fries, you'll be going home with a full belly and a full wallet. You cannot beat this place on value.
